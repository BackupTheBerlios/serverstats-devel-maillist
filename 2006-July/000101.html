> We must move with the times ... ;)
> Ajax is fashion,  I use it with my xforms engine and works very well !

But Serverstats is not about fashion, it is about KISS ("keep it small
and simple"). Thats the reason why I created Serverstats (Cacti was so
bloated) and I think that should stay one of the main goals.

So, like I said: JS can extend the script, but it should not be the only
way to navigate through graphs. Perhaps some JS could take the layout,
add AJAX to it, but keep the current html intact.

I like AJAX and love to use websites that take advantage of it, but I
dislike creating webpages that are not browseable without JS. Perhaps
thats one of the things you care about if you start having a
community[1] of webdevs and always need to tell them: "Don't use JS
here, you will keep 35%[2] of your possible visitors from using your
homepage".
[1] http://www.webmasterpro.de/ (german)
[2] according to the statistics calculated by all users (about 122000)
of the Flashcounter (http://fc.webmasterpro.de/)
